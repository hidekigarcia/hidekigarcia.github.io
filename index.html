HIDEKI GARCIA GOO

M.A. Psychology, B.S. Electrical Engineering

I am a Master’s student in the Department of Psychology at the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley (UT-RGV). Since Fall 2018, I have been working with Dr. Megan Strait (initially as an undergraduate research assistant and now graduate research assistant) to study the reproduction and reinforcement of gendered and racialized marginalization via emergent forms of social interaction including social media, artificial intelligences, and humanlike robots.

I am interested in researching social bias, its consequences, and its manifestation across traditional forms of interaction (e.g., face-to-face) and technology-based interaction modalities that have emerged. I am especially interested in the transfer to and reproduction of bias in people’s interactions with agentic technologies (e.g., robots) and the capacity of such systems to address, deflect, and/or mitigate relational aggression in people’s interactions with other people.
