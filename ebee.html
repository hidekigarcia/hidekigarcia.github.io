ebee

Using Negative Affect to Reinforce Moral Norms in Casual Speech

Motivation

Inspired by the political climate and instances of political action from social move ments during COVID-19, we created “ebee”; a robot with the purpose of helping us use a more inclusive language.

Ebee works by letting you know when you use words from your personal dictionary that contributes to the further marginalization of vulnerable population. You will be able to add your own words, or add them from a provided list that includes the reason why they are recommended. Ebee will light up its eyes when it hears you say a word from the list and make record that you used it to report it to you at the end of the day.

Design Process

Hardware

After comming up with the initial design of ebee, we decided to proceed to 3D print it. We used Fusion 360 for the 3D sketch and printed it at the InnoVaqueros Makerspace using a Makerbot Replicator+ printer.

Fusion 360 3D model.
Ebee once it was 3D printed and painted.

For the hardware components we used a Raspberry Pi 4, Adafruit NeoPixel and speaker cloth for the eyes, and a mic. When ebee recognizes a word in the user's list it will "wake up" to let the user know they used it.

App

In the app, the user will be able to access the list of words that will activate ebee when they are used, a global list of curated words that we recommend to be added along with why, and a space for the user to add their own words.

We developed a mobile application using that connects to ebee, and allows the user to input the words that they would like to "blacklist". Along with this dictionary, the application has a homepage with an introduction to ebee, an about page where we describe our motivation, and a contact page.
